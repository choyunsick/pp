The need for exercise

Every January, many people visit the gym. In the new year, people decide to exercise and challenge with a new mindset, but not many people succeed in the challenge. What can be gained through exercise is various such as health, body shape management, achievement, and pleasure. The ultimate goal of exercising is health care, which is very effective, but is often ignorant of exactly how to exercise. Usually, exercise is largely divided into aerobic exercise and anaerobic exercise, which refers to continuous exercise without stopping for at least 20 minutes. Typical examples include walking, cycling, swimming, mountain climbing, and soccer.

An example of a sense of accomplishment

3.9km swimming, 180.2km cycling, and 42.2km running. This is the "Iron Man" course, which is the most difficult among triathlon. It is a breathtaking process that ordinary people cannot dare to dare to overcome human limitations. In fact, even those who exercise cannot hastily challenge without sufficient preparation. However, there is a person who participates in such a fierce game every month. Kim Dong-wook (46), director of Dell Korea, who stepped into the triathlon four years ago, is the main character. As triathlon is not an exercise that anyone can do, there is a considerable sense of accomplishment and joy when they achieve it. Director Kim's first game was the "Icheon Seolbong Iron Triathlon" held in Icheon, Gyeonggi-do in July 2008. It was a time full of tension, excitement, and fear. Therefore, it is said that he still vividly remembers how he felt at that time. "When you finish, you can't even talk about joy. I felt like my heart was going to explode with the sense of accomplishment and joy that I finally did it. But I was terrified throughout the game. It's a particularly difficult course for swimming. Swimming is the most burdensome thing not only for me but also for other participants. When I practice, I don't have fear because I do it in a swimming pool with a clear view of the floor, but in triathlon, I get scared because I do it in rivers, lakes, or seas where I can't see the bottom at all. Also, the water temperature suddenly changes, so I often give up halfway. After swimming, bicycles and marathons are waiting, especially on that day, the rain was drizzling, and all kinds of worries were filled in my head, "Would the bicycle slip and get hurt?" and "Would a heart attack while running?" (laughs). But every time I did that, I was able to endure 17 hours (a kind of image training) imagining myself taking a warm shower and drinking cold beer after the game."
